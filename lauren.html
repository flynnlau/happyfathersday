My sister is fearless. Even though she has not touched a wheel in years, she can throw enormous bowls and vases. I throw cups. Fearlessness is not my strength.

Patience could be. I recently heard patience defined as “caring slowly.”

I have been thinking that I want art to be more like life. I am not talking about shifting where art happens or who makes it, though these things are of course important. I am talking about the ways time fits with space and how we come to tend their corners.

I am thinking about the temporality of water and of vegetables, of cooking for one, for two, for six, for twelve.
